Story Starter Cards
Each card gives a setting, two characters, and a conflict. Cut them out and keep them in a box. When a child is stuck for ideas, pull a card and start building! Perfect for Week 2 and Week 6.
✂️ Print, cut out each card
📦 Keep in a story box or envelope
🎲 Pull one randomly when stuck
✏️ Fill in the "Your Twist" section
📱 Then open ScratchJr and build it!
6 story cards + 1 blank card to fill in yourself · Print on A4, cut along the card borders

Character Design Sheet
Before drawing in ScratchJr, kids design their character on paper first — just like professional game designers. Name, personality, colours, emotions. Included: one completed example + two blank sheets to print.
💡
Why paper first?Professional game designers always sketch characters before coding. It's faster to change on paper than to redraw in the app.
🎨
Best for Week 1 & 6Use before Day 1 (creating first character) and before Week 6 Day 2 (art day). Makes the tablet session much smoother.
✂️
How to usePrint 2–3 sheets. Child fills in name, personality, and colours. Then draws character in the big box. Then opens ScratchJr.
Prints 1 sheet per page · Print 2–3 copies per child · Works in black & white too

Storyboard Template
A 3-panel planning sheet for the Week 6 final project — or any multi-scene project. Kids sketch each scene, write what happens, and tick which ScratchJr blocks they'll need. Plan before you build — every great project starts here.
🎬
Used by real filmmakersPixar, Disney, and every game studio uses storyboards. Now your child does too.
📅
Best for Week 6 Day 1Use on the Big Idea Planning Day. Fill it in before touching ScratchJr. Saves hours of confusion later.
📄
Print 2 copiesFirst copy for rough ideas. Second copy for the final plan once they've decided on their story.
One sheet = one scene sequence · Print 2 copies (rough + final) · Works perfectly in black & white
