🔧 Troubleshooting Guide + 📋 Progress Checklist

For parents & home teachers — quick fixes, observation tools, and weekly tracking

🔧 Common Problems & Fixes
😤
"Nothing is happening when I press play!"
Likely causes:
1No trigger block — check if the script starts with a yellow block (green flag or tap)
2Blocks aren't snapped together — tap and drag to re-connect them
3Wrong character selected — make sure you're editing the character you want to move
"What does the FIRST block in your script look like? Is it yellow?"
😵
"My character runs off the screen and disappears!"
Likely causes:
1Move number is too high — reduce from 10 to 3 or 4
2Direction keeps going one way — alternate left/right to keep it on screen
3Forever loop going one direction — character will always exit eventually
"What number makes it go a little bit? What number makes it go TOO much?"
📨
"My message isn't working — the second character won't react!"
Likely causes:
1Message colours don't match — sender and receiver MUST be the same colour dot
2Receiver has no trigger — it needs a "when I receive [colour]" block at the start
3Receiver script is on the wrong character — double-check which character you're editing
"What colour is the SEND block? What colour is the RECEIVE block? Are they matching?"
🔁
"My loop runs too fast / I can't see what's happening!"
Likely causes:
1Add a small WAIT block (1-2 taps) inside the loop to slow it down
2Reduce movement speed (lower numbers on move blocks)
3This is actually fine! Fast loops create smooth animations
"Do you want it to be fast like a machine or slow like a dance? Let's try both!"
📄
"My page jump doesn't go to the right page!"
Likely causes:
1Check the number in the "go to page" block — is it the right page number?
2Pages are in the wrong order in the tray — drag to reorder
3Trigger isn't at the very beginning of the script
"Which page do you WANT to jump to? What number is that page in the list?"
😩
"I deleted something by accident and can't get it back!"
Quick fix:
1Shake the device — ScratchJr has an undo shake feature!
2If not available: breathe. Rebuilding is practice — most kids do it faster the second time
3Save more often! Teach the habit: "build a bit, save a bit"
"That's frustrating! Can you remember what it looked like? Let's rebuild it together — I bet it takes half the time!"
🎤
"The voice recording doesn't save / can't hear it!"
Quick fix:
1Check microphone permissions — go to device Settings → ScratchJr → allow microphone
2Record in a quiet space — background noise can cause recording issues
3Speak louder and closer to the mic — hold the device 20cm from the mouth
"Let's try recording in a quieter spot — and speak like you're telling the whole class!"
🧠
"The child is frustrated and wants to quit!"
This is totally normal — here's your toolkit:
1Step away from the tablet for 5 minutes — physical movement helps reset
2Reduce scope: "Let's just make ONE thing work today instead of everything"
3Switch to drawing: "Let's design the characters and come back to code"
4Validate the feeling: "Debugging is the hardest part. Even professional programmers feel this!"
"What ONE small part would you like to get working? Just one thing."
⚡
"The child is bored — flying through the material!"
Extension challenges:
1Add more characters — "Can you make 5 things happen at the same time?"
2Make it interactive — "Can a viewer make choices in your story?"
3Combine skills from multiple weeks in one project
4Let them teach YOU — "Show me how you did that. Pretend I'm a new student."
"You've mastered this! Now make it THREE TIMES harder or more interesting. What would that look like?"

💬 What to Say Instead — Teacher Mindset Phrases
🐛
When something breaks
✅ "Ooh, interesting! Something unexpected happened. Let's be detectives — what did you WANT to happen?"
❌ "Let me fix that for you." / "You did it wrong."
😤
When they're frustrated
✅ "Debugging is the hardest part — even professionals feel this. What ONE small thing can we fix right now?"
❌ "It's easy, look..." / "Don't worry, I'll do it."
⭐
When they succeed
✅ "You figured that out! How did you know to try that?" (Praise the process, not the result)
❌ "Good job!" (too generic — doesn't build growth mindset)
🤔
When they're stuck
✅ "What have you tried so far? What's one thing you HAVEN'T tried yet?"
❌ Immediately pointing to the answer or clicking for them.
💡
When they have a big idea
✅ "I love that! What's the FIRST step to make that happen? Just the first one."
❌ "That might be too complicated." / "Let's do something simpler."
🏁
End of each session
✅ "What's one thing you made today that didn't exist this morning? That's incredible."
❌ Ending without any reflection or celebration of progress.
